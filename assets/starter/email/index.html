Email

Send transactional email through WordPress (wp_mail) without ever seeing the recipient address. The bridge resolves "admin" or "current_user" server-side.

Real send Clicking “Send” below dispatches an actual email via the site’s mail transport. If the site has no SMTP configured, mails may go to spam or fail silently. Use sparingly.

Live: dsgo.email.send({ to, subject, body })

Permission: email · manifest must declare email.recipients

This starter declares recipients: ["admin"], so to: "current_user" would be rejected.

No send yet.

await window.dsgo.email.send({
  to:      'admin',           // or 'current_user' (must be in manifest)
  subject: 'Notification',
  body:    'Plain text only — no HTML, no headers.',
});
What happens when you call this The bridge looks up the resolved address (admin email, or the logged-in user’s email), validates it, and calls wp_mail(). If to is "current_user" and the visitor is anonymous, you get not_authenticated. If to isn’t in your manifest’s email.recipients, you get permission_denied.
